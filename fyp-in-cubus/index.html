FYP: IN//CUBUS

With my final year project entitled IN//CUBUS I want to play with human curiosity, our need for exploring unfamiliar environments and co-operation through an interactive art installation.

It all starts by putting an unfamiliar, alien-looking object into a space that will seemingly try to attract your attention. Rather than a tangible interface I want to give the gallery visitor means to look inside the mysterious objects using (their own) mobile phones to make for a more intimate experience that will then in turn attract more visitors.

What I want to explore with the installation is how I can get a small group of people (who may or may not be acquainted with one another) to cooperate for a mutual goal and test how long it would take them to realise that there is no way of reaching it and the cube’s purpose was to bring them together all along.

Essentially, I just described 22cans’ curiosity minus the global reach of the internet. However, my main inspiration for the this project roots in the science-fiction thriller cube in which a group of specialised individuals wake up in a cubic maze with no recollection of how they got there. Whilst traversing the maze looking for a way out, the group encounters trap rooms, some which cannot be avoided.

Secondly the first multiplayer instance of the Legend of Zelda series, where the puzzles in-game adjust to the amount of players (up to four). Ideally the rooms will be procedurally generated at the start of every session so no two groups of players will ever get the same experience.

Whilst the Zelda games are renowned for encouraging exploration, the art style will not necessarily fit in with the minimalistic appearance of the sculpture. This is where the project ties in beautifully with my dissertation, in which I attempt to come up with a framework for telling rich stories through a constrained visual language.

What I do know is that to me personally it is not important to come up with a commercially viable product, I leave that to (product) design students. What I want to end up with is a piece that defines me as an artist.
